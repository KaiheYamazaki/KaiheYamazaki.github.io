...So, why the Antarctic ocean?

Southern Ocean drives the global ocean circulation

The Southern Ocean plays a fundamental role in the global climate system, particularly in producing the densest and largest seawater on the Earth: Antarctic Bottom Water. The formation of bottom water is the dynamical driver of the global Meridional Overturning Circulation (MOC). In the Southern Ocean, surface water sinks to the abyssal layer, whereas deep water upwells close the MOC circuit, just like a hub of the global ocean. The MOC is substantial for the global redistribution of heat and carbon.

Schematic of MOC. From Descriptive Physical Oceanography 6th edition.

IPCC AR5 reported that the deep ocean has warmed over the past several decades, with the most significant warming in the Southern Ocean. This change is remarkable in the tranquil abyssal ocean; however, its cause yet to be disclosed.

Antarctica: the largest uncertainity in the future sea-level projection

Ice is fundamental for the climate system. The Southern Ocean is adjoining to the Antarctic ice sheet, a giant reservoir of freshwater equivalent to 60 m elevation of sea level. Trials to predict future sea-level rise are ongoing. However, there is still a significant uncertainty due to the complexity of the ice sheets response to the oceanic condition. IPCC SROCC would be good to know our findings as a first step.

Southern Ocean drives the global ocean circulation

The Southern Ocean plays the fundamental role for the global climate system, particulary in producing the densest and largest seawater in the world: Antarctic Bottom Water, as surface water tends to get extremely cold. The formation of bottom water is currently assumed as one of the dynamical driver of the Meridional Overturning Circulation (MOC). In the Southern Ocean, seawater does not only sink, but also upwell so as to close the circuit of MOC, as like a hub of the global ocean. The MOC is important for globally transporting heat and carbon.

According to IPCC, it is likely that the bottom seawater has warmed for the past decades, with the largest warming in the Southern Ocean. This change is quite drastic in the tranquil abyssal ocean, however, this cause yet to be revealed.

The rapid melt of the Antarctic land ice

Ice is also fundamental for the climate system. The Southern Ocean is adjoining to the Antarctic ice sheet, which is a giant reservoir of fresh water equivalent to 60 m elevation of sea-level. Trials to predict the future sea-level rise are ongoing, but there still exists large uncertainity due to the complexity of the dynamical responce of the ice sheets to the oceanic condition.
