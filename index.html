A POINT OF VIEW

The Earth is a planet of water. Since water has remarkable heat capacity among substances on the Earth, the ocean is in charge of the main driver of the climate system (by timescale from seasons to thousands of years).

What should be noticed is that the Southern Ocean holds great ambiguity primely due to its inaccessibility and dynamical complexity, which are associated with ice, despite its great importance in the global climate. Water originating there, Antarctic Bottom Water (AABW) is the coldest, densest, and largest watermass in the world ocean. Formation of AABW is assumed to modulate the planetary scale heat and material transportation.
