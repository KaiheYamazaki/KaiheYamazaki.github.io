MOTIVATION

To understand the climate variability is the core of our survival strategy.

Regarding the climate system, the polar region contains substantial ambiguity, primely due to its inaccessibility and dynamical complexity, associated with ice. Especially, the Antarctic ocean plays a key role for global climate by two parts.

The first is to control sea level; on going global wamring enhances ice shelf melting and abruptly raises sea level, quite timely. The second is as an originating region of the global ocean circulation; Antarctic Bottom Water (AABW) is the coldest, densest, and largest water mass in the world ocean. It is believed that formation of heavy AABW drives the meridional overturning circulation and modulates planetary scale heat transport.

My commitment is to contribute to the knowledge of the Antarctic ocean and the climate system.

INTERESTS
